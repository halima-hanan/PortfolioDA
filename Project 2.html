Project Overview

Background

This project analyzes RevoBank’s credit card performance over the past six months. The bank generates revenue through a 1.5% Merchant Discount Rate (MDR) on non-fraud transactions. The analysis seeks to identify potential customer segments to optimize profitability while managing financial risk across different card brands and user demographics.

Method

  • data cleaning, including type conversion, handling typos in card brands, and standardizing missing values to zero
  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) followed, examining fraud rates, card brand behavior, and user risk profiles
  • K-Means clustering model was implemented, utilizing the elbow and silhouette methods to determine optimal segments

Result

  • RevoBank recorded a net profit of IDR 5,639,860,570, with a low fraud rate of 0.3%
  • Analysis identified Cluster 0 (Inactive High Credit Users) as having the highest profit potential due to moderate DTI (0.29) and high credit limits; therefore, the bank should implement activation campaigns and usage reminders for this specific segment to drive growth
