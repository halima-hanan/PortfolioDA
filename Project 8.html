Project Overview

Background

OCA is a CPaaS platform that enables businesses to send messages through WhatsApp, SMS, Email, and Call. However, active users contribute unevenly to total messages and revenue, making it difficult for the company to decide which users should receive retention, nurturing, or upsell treatment. Without clear segmentation, all users may be treated the same, which can make personalization, churn prevention, and upsell strategies less effective. This project analyzes user behavior to identify high-value users, early decline signals, and potential growth opportunities.

Method

  • Conducted business understanding to define the need for user segmentation, retention signal detection, and upsell opportunity identification.
  • Prepared user-level transaction data from 1 January – 31 March 2025 across WhatsApp, SMS, Email, and Call channels.
  • Built a rule-based segmentation framework to classify users into Strategic Users, Growth Users, and Low-Scale Users.
  • Added secondary labels: Retention Signal to detect early signs of declining activity and Upsell Readiness to assess users’ potential for upgrade opportunities.
  • Developed a Segment Priority Score using revenue contribution, revenue at risk, recoverable revenue per user, and at-risk user rate to prioritize business actions.

Result

  • Segmented active users into Strategic Users, Growth Users, and Low-Scale Users based on message volume and revenue contribution.
  • Found that Strategic Users were the most impactful segment, contributing 74.97% of total revenue despite consisting of only 6 users.
  • Estimated that if the current decline continues, Strategic Users could contribute Rp1,309,549 in revenue at risk over three months.
  • Prioritized Strategic Users as P1 with a Segment Priority Score of 1.00, followed by Growth Users as P2 with 0.234, and Low-Scale Users as P3.
  • Recommended focusing on revenue protection, account manager follow-up, business review, and usage recovery support for Strategic Users before pursuing aggressive upsell initiatives.
