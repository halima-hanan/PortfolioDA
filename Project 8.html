Project Overview

Background

QuickU established five new distribution hubs in January 2022, creating challenges in maintaining optimal inventory levels due to unstable demand patterns and the absence of a structured forecasting system. This project analyzed historical sales data from January–July 2022 to develop a data-driven stock planning strategy for August 2022, aiming to reduce stockout and overstock risks while improving operational efficiency.

Method

  • Performed time series decomposition to identify demand trends and weekly seasonality patterns across 10 product categories
  • Built forecasting models using Holt-Winters Exponential Smoothing and SARIMA to estimate weekly demand for August 2022
  • Evaluated model performance using MAPE and standard error metrics to select the most reliable forecasting approach
  • Calculated safety stock, reorder point, and recommendation stock to support risk-aware inventory planning
  • Developed a risk-based prioritization framework by combining demand volume, variability, and stockout risk factors

Result

  • Identified an increasing demand trend with recurring demand spikes in week 4 across multiple product categories
  • Revealed that demand variability contributed more significantly to inventory risk than total sales volume
  • Enabled proactive inventory planning through dynamic safety stock and reorder point calculations
  • Supported more efficient resource allocation by prioritizing high-risk product categories with greater stockout potential
  • Shifted inventory management from reactive estimation methods to a structured, data-driven stock planning system
