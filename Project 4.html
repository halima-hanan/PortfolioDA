Scope of Work

  • Analyzed 2017 sales data using Power Pivot to identify a 59.96 billion total revenue and peak monthly performance
  • Evaluated category performance to determine that Gaun and Atasan are the most popular, contributing 32.60% and 26.03% respectively
  • Identified Jaket as the top-selling individual product with 7.64 billion in revenue, outperforming lower-demand items like socks
  • Assessed regional performance across four branches, pinpointing Medan as the top performer and Surabaya as having the highest growth potential

Project Overview

Background

DQFashion is a fashion retail startup in Indonesia operating an e-commerce platform for local brands. This project analyzes the company's 2017 sales data to address underutilized data resources. The analysis provides a systematic evaluation of sales patterns, top products, and branch performance to support data-driven decision-making.

Method

  • data preparation and cleaning using Power Query to handle the DQFashion 2017 dataset
  • Data modeling was performed with Power Pivot to establish relationships between master tables for products, categories, branches, and sales transactions
  • trends and category performances were analyzed to derive actionable insights

Result

  • The analysis revealed a total revenue of 59.96 billion, with a significant peak in June (7.94 billion) driven by Idul Fitri
  • Insights highlight Gaun as the highest revenue category at 32.60%
  • recommendations to optimize peak-period marketing and evaluate low-margin categories like Accessories through seasonal bundling
