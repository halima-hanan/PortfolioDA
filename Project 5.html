Scope of Work

  • Measuring portfolio health using OS, ENR, and TKB30 metrics

  • Analyzing risk factors based on: Customer profile & Loan characteristics
  • Identifying gaps in risk management and underwriting processes
  • Providing data-driven recommendations to improve portfolio quality

Project Overview

Background

RevoFin is a fintech lending company with a growing loan portfolio. As the number of loans increases, credit risk also increases, necessitating analysis to understand portfolio health and risk distribution. This project aims to evaluate loan portfolio performance and identify high-risk customer cohorts and segments to support data-driven decision-making in the lending process.

Method

  • Perform data cleaning and standardization of customer_id using BigQuery to ensure data consistency.
  • Define active loan portfolio based on loan status (Current, Grace Period, Late, Default).
  • Calculate key metrics: Outstanding (OS), ENR (End Net Receivable), TKB30 (Loan performance metric).
  • Conduct cohort analysis based on loan issuance time to identify risk concentrations.
  • Conduct deep-dive analysis of cohort anomalies by comparing: Customer profile & Loan characteristics.

Result

  • Identified anomaly cohorts with lower portfolio performance, where average TKB30 declined from 98.25% to 96.62%, indicating increased >30-day delinquency risk.
  • Found that portfolio deterioration was primarily driven by higher exposure to debt consolidation loans (56.03% distribution; 2.67% bad rate vs 1.70%) rather than borrower demographic differences.
  • Detected higher concentration of 60-month loan tenors in anomaly cohorts (35.57% vs 31.75%) and consistently higher bad rates across all pricing tiers, indicating concentration risk and potential underwriting gaps.
  • Delivered recommendations for stricter underwriting, enhanced monitoring of high-risk segments, and improved risk segmentation to support healthier portfolio performance.
